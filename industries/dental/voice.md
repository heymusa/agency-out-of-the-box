# Voice — Dental Marketing

Voice guide loaded automatically when `agency-context.md §2 niche: dental`. Every page-gen skill reads this before writing copy.

## Tone

**Direct, practitioner-respectful, numerate.** Dentists are clinicians who run small businesses. They've been pitched by every "growth hacker" and "marketing guru" on the planet. They detect bullshit on first read. The right voice is what a respected peer with marketing chops would say at a CE event over coffee — informed, specific, slightly dry, no cheerleading.

## Three do-words

1. **Practitioner-respectful** — assume they know dentistry. Don't explain hygiene capacity or PPO economics to them.
2. **Numerate** — ground every claim in a number, timeframe, or named workflow ("recall," "case acceptance," "treatment plan presentation," "FFS conversion").
3. **Boring-on-purpose** — confidence comes from specificity, not energy. The opposite of LinkedIn-influencer voice.

## Three don't-words

1. **Not "passionate"** — never use the word. Or "dedicated" or "innovative." See `rules/conversion.md` §3.
2. **Not "growth hacker"** — bro-y, hyped, juvenile. Buyer disqualifier.
3. **Not patient-facing** — we're talking to the dentist-owner, not the patient. Avoid "your bright smile" or "your dental health journey."

## Vocabulary

### Use these terms (industry-correct)

- **Production** — total fee revenue (not "sales").
- **Collections** — what was actually collected post-insurance.
- **Case acceptance** — % of treatment plans accepted.
- **Hygiene re-care / recall** — patient retention via hygiene visits.
- **Net new patient** — first-time patients who book a treatment beyond initial cleaning.
- **FFS / fee-for-service** — out-of-network, full-fee patients.
- **PPO** — preferred provider organization (insurance contracts).
- **Operatories / ops** — treatment chairs (e.g., "12-op practice").
- **Hygienist capacity** — hours of hygienist availability per week.
- **Doctor time** — hours available for restorative.
- **Schedule density** — how full the schedule is week-over-week.
- **Treatment plan presentation** — the conversation post-exam where treatment is recommended.
- **GBP / Google Business Profile** — formerly GMB.
- **NAP consistency** — Name/Address/Phone uniformity across directories.
- **Reviews velocity** — rate of new reviews per month.

### Avoid these terms (red flags)

- **"Bright smile"** — patient-facing copy. Wrong audience.
- **"Dental practice growth"** — vague. Replace with specific metric ("new patient volume," "case acceptance rate," "collections").
- **"Comprehensive marketing solution"** — buzzword stack. See `rules/conversion.md`.
- **"Cutting-edge technology"** — every dentist's website says this. Differentiates nothing.
- **"Family-friendly"** — a service trait, not a marketing claim.
- **"Smile makeover specialists"** — patient-facing, copy-paste-ish.

## Sample sentences

### Right voice (use this kind)

> "We add 8-12 fee-for-service or PPO-out-of-network new patients per month to your practice. No Groupon, no $99 specials that erode case acceptance. The mechanism is local SEO + reactivation + paid-search on commercial-intent terms that actually convert."

> "Most practices we work with are doing $1.2M-$3.8M and stuck — hygiene at 92%+ but new patient volume below 30/mo. The gap is almost always one of two things: weak Google Business Profile + thin organic presence, or a leaky reactivation funnel for inactive patients."

> "Our retainer is $3,500/mo. First 30 days delivers a GBP rebuild, reactivation campaign to your inactive patient list, and a Google Ads cohort live by day 14. If you don't see at least 5 net-new patient inquiries by day 30, we refund the setup and you owe nothing else."

### Wrong voice (rewrite)

> ❌ "We're a passionate team dedicated to revolutionizing dental practice growth with our cutting-edge marketing solutions."

> ❌ "Imagine your practice transformed by our holistic approach to patient acquisition."

> ❌ "Unlock the full potential of your practice with our world-class digital strategies."

These all fail the buzzword scan in `rules/conversion.md` §3.

## CTA voice

Specific, value-bearing, peer-tone:

- "Book my 20-min strategy call"
- "Get my dental SEO + GBP audit"
- "See pricing for my practice size"
- "Show me the case studies (12 practices, 9 states)"

Reject:
- "Submit"
- "Get started"
- "Learn more"
- "Click here for your free consultation today"

## Rhetorical question patterns

When opening a page or section that addresses pain:

- "Tired of chasing dental insurance reimbursements that took 90 days to net you 65% of the bill?"
- "Hygiene chairs sitting empty Tuesdays and Wednesdays?"
- "Net new patients dropped 30% since 2023 and Google Ads CPL keeps climbing?"

These work because they're **specific** to dental — the reader thinks "yes, exactly that," not "vague generic pain."

## Imagery voice (referenced from imagery.md)

Real photos of:
- The owner-dentist in their actual practice (in scrubs, with team, with patient — with consent).
- The team during morning huddle.
- The actual operatory equipment.

Never:
- Stock photos of stereotypically beautiful patients.
- AI-generated dentist with a giant white smile.
- Vague "team of professionals" stock.

## Compliance touches

The voice is also constrained by ABA / state dental advertising rules — see `compliance.md`. Key rule: don't make claims about being "the best" or "#1" — most state dental boards prohibit superlative advertising.
