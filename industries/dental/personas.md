## Decision-maker — the Owner-Dentist (primary)

### Demographic
- Age: 38-58 (5-25 years post-DDS).
- Owns a single-location practice or a 2-3 location small group.
- 8-25 employees including hygienists, assistants, front desk.
- Practice annual collections: $800K to $5M.
- Lives in or near the practice's market.

### What they care about (in priority order)
1. **Schedule density.** Empty chairs = bleeding money. Hygiene at 95%+ AND doctor time at 80%+ is the goal.
2. **Case acceptance rate.** % of treatment plans the patient says yes to. 65% is good; 75%+ is excellent.
3. **Net new patient volume.** Per month, segmented by FFS / PPO-in / PPO-out / Medicaid.
4. **Patient retention.** Active patient list growth vs. attrition.
5. **Production per hour.** Doctor production + hygiene production.
6. **Time at home.** Most owner-dentists are working IN the practice 50+ hours/wk and want out.

### What they don't care about
- "Brand awareness." Vague metric, no business value.
- "Social media followers." Doesn't fill chairs.
- Ad creative that "tells our story." Wants results.
- Fancy reporting dashboards. Wants "did our chair-time fill up this month."

### How they buy
- Long memory for being burned. Most have been pitched by 5-15 marketing agencies / consultants over their career.
- Trust referrals from peer dentists more than any pitch.
- Read Dental Economics, AGD's Impact, and ADA News. Subscribe to Mark Costes' Dentalpreneur podcast and Howard Farran's Dentaltown.
- Attend AGD, ADA, AAID, ASDA conferences.
- Sign solo. No procurement committee.
- Take 1-3 calls before signing. First call is qualifying; second is offer; third (if needed) is "what about [specific concern]."

### Common objections we'll need to handle
- **"I've been burned before by [previous agency]."** Address with specifics: how we work, no long contracts, money-back-on-setup.
- **"My PMS is [Dentrix / Eaglesoft / Open Dental] — can you integrate?"** Yes for Dentrix and Open Dental v1; Eaglesoft on roadmap.
- **"My PMS won't let me do reactivation campaigns easily."** Show our reactivation process — we extract the inactive list, run the campaign, route inquiries to the front desk's existing intake workflow.
- **"What about HIPAA?"** We're HIPAA-aware (see compliance.md). No PHI in marketing platforms.
- **"My brother-in-law's wife runs my Facebook page."** No problem — we handle the channels she doesn't (SEO, GBP, paid search, reactivation). The org chart is honored.

### Voice notes
- Refer to them as "Dr. {LastName}" in copy and in proposals. Not "Dr. {FirstName}" until they invite first-name.
- They are clinicians first, business owners second. Respect both.

## Secondary stakeholder — the Practice Manager / Office Manager

### Demographic
- Age: 32-55.
- Often a former hygienist or front-desk lead.
- Reports to the owner-dentist.
- Manages: scheduling, insurance verification, hiring, inventory, sometimes books.

### What they care about
1. **Front-desk workload.** New marketing inquiries that route through their existing intake without breaking it.
2. **Reporting clarity.** A simple weekly recap of "what came in, what booked, what didn't."
3. **Their own time.** Not having to learn 4 new tools.

### How they influence the deal
- The owner-dentist often forwards proposals to them for "tell me what you think."
- A skeptical practice manager can kill a deal even if the dentist likes us. Earn their trust early.
- Write the section "What you bring / What we handle" so they immediately see we're not adding work.

### Voice notes
- Respect their operational expertise. Don't talk down. They're often the most informed person in the practice.

## Anti-persona — who we don't sell to

### The Corporate-Practice DSO (Dental Service Organization)
- 10+ locations under a brand (Aspen Dental, Heartland Dental, Pacific Dental Services, Smile Brands).
- Has internal marketing team.
- Procurement-driven; long sales cycles; 6-month security review.
- Margins on engagement are thin.
- **Why not:** unit economics fail for a small / mid agency.

### The Solo Dentist Doing < $500K
- Single-doctor, often part-time or near retirement.
- Small budget; high attention demand; mismatch on what we charge.
- **Why not:** they need a $500-1000/mo marketing tool, not a $3500/mo retainer. Refer them to local-SEO DIY tools.

### The Corporate Group with a Junior Partner Looking for "Help"
- Junior partner reaches out without owner authority.
- Conversation goes 4 calls and dies in committee.
- **Why not:** no authority, no deal.

### The Dentist Who Says "I Just Need a New Website"
- Wants a $5k website project.
- Doesn't want SEO, ads, or retention work.
- **Why not:** we don't do one-and-done websites. Refer to a local web designer.

## Trigger events that bring them to us

The "why now" — what changed in their world that makes them search:

1. **New-patient drop month-over-month.** Often Q4-Q1 transition or post-Google-update.
2. **A competitor opened nearby.** Especially a corporate (Aspen / Heartland) within 3-5 miles.
3. **Insurance contract change.** PPO carrier reduced reimbursements; need to attract more FFS patients.
4. **Acquisition / retirement transition.** Buying a practice, selling, or absorbing a retiring dentist's panel.
5. **Hygiene capacity expansion.** Hired a new hygienist; need to fill the new chair.
6. **Failed previous marketer.** Spent $20k+ with someone, got nothing.

The hero copy on the agency site should resonate with at least 2-3 of these triggers above the fold.
