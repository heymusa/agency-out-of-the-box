# Scoring Rubric — Full Reference

Each dimension scored 1-5. Sum to 40. <28 is a hard pass. 28-32 workable. 33+ strong.

## 1. Buyer authority

How many people need to say yes before money moves?

- **5** — Solo decision. Owner or sole partner. Can sign on the call. (Local dentist, plumber owner, solo lawyer.)
- **4** — Two-person decision. Owner + spouse, owner + ops manager.
- **3** — Small committee (3-5). Department head + finance + IT.
- **2** — Mid-market procurement (5-10 stakeholders).
- **1** — Enterprise procurement (RFP, legal, security review). Long sales cycles. Cash flow killer for small agencies.

Service agencies under $1M ARR should never go below 3 on this dimension.

## 2. Average deal size

Project minimum / retainer minimum.

- **5** — $25k+ projects, $5k+/mo retainers. (Personal injury law SEO, fractional CFO firms, B2B SaaS demand-gen.)
- **4** — $10k-25k projects, $2k-5k/mo retainers. (Most dental marketing, legal SEO mid-tier.)
- **3** — $5k-10k projects, $1k-2k/mo retainers. (Local SEO for home services.)
- **2** — $2k-5k projects, $500-1k/mo retainers.
- **1** — Sub-$2k projects, sub-$500/mo retainers. (Doesn't pay you. Move on.)

## 3. Recurring revenue potential

Does the service have natural retainer attachment?

- **5** — Clear retainer model: SEO (ongoing), paid ads management (ongoing), social content (ongoing), email marketing (ongoing).
- **4** — Project + retainer hybrid: site build → ongoing SEO retainer.
- **3** — Some recurring possible: occasional refresh / quarterly review.
- **2** — Mostly project-based with occasional re-engagement.
- **1** — Pure one-and-done. (Single websites with no SEO/ads attached. Difficult.)

## 4. Pain urgency

How often does the buyer feel the pain?

- **5** — Daily / weekly. (No leads coming in for a roofer in storm season; 100 calls a day for a dental insurance call center.)
- **4** — Monthly. (Reporting deadlines, monthly KPI reviews.)
- **3** — Quarterly. (Quarterly board reports, seasonal campaigns.)
- **2** — Yearly. (Annual marketing budget cycle.)
- **1** — Latent. (Buyer doesn't realize there's a problem until you tell them.) Long sales cycle.

## 5. Solution legibility

Can the buyer understand what you sell without education?

- **5** — Obvious. "We do Google Ads for plumbers." "We rank dentists on Google Maps."
- **4** — Mostly obvious with one short sentence of clarification.
- **3** — Needs a 5-minute pitch.
- **2** — Needs a 30-minute discovery call to explain.
- **1** — Needs an industry primer plus a discovery call.

Higher = lower CAC. Skills that need a primer always have higher CAC.

## 6. Lead-gen efficiency

CPL on Google / Meta vs LTV.

- **5** — CPL < 1% of LTV. Cheap, high-intent search terms exist.
- **4** — CPL 1-3% of LTV.
- **3** — CPL 3-5% of LTV.
- **2** — CPL 5-10% of LTV.
- **1** — CPL > 10% of LTV. Burns ad spend. Look for partnership-led or content-led GTM instead.

## 7. Geographic concentration

Do buyers cluster in identifiable places?

- **5** — Highly clustered. Conferences, trade associations, directories, geographic zips. (Dental conventions, ABA bar associations, contractor licensing boards.)
- **4** — Some clustering. LinkedIn groups, Slack communities, niche newsletters.
- **3** — Spread but reachable via specific media.
- **2** — Diffuse. Have to go broad to reach.
- **1** — Random. No common watering hole.

Higher = lower CAC and easier referral motion.

## 8. Competitor density

Goldilocks zone: 3-15 visible competitors per geography.

- **5** — 5-10 visible competitors. Healthy demand, room for differentiation.
- **4** — 3-5 OR 10-15 competitors. Good but watch positioning.
- **3** — 15-25 competitors. Crowded but workable.
- **2** — 0-2 OR 25-50 competitors. Either no demand OR red ocean.
- **1** — 0 competitors with no obvious reason → demand probably doesn't exist; or 50+ → race to the bottom on price.

## How to actually score

Sit down with the niche, open the rubric, score each dimension honestly. The mistake is to inflate scores because you've fallen in love with a niche on vibes. Inflated scores produce the same result every time: 6 months of effort, no traction, pivot.

If you can't honestly score 4+ on **at least** 5 dimensions, look for another niche.
