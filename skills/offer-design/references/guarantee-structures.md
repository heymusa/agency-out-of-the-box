# Guarantee Structures — 8 Templates

A guarantee is the single biggest lever on perceived likelihood. Pick the structure that matches the offer's risk profile and what the operator can actually defend.

## 1. Conditional money-back

**Structure:** "If [specific measurable outcome] doesn't happen by [date], you get a full refund."

**When to use:** Outcome is in your control AND measurable in 30-90 days.

**Sample copy:**
> "If we don't deliver at least 5 net-new qualified patient inquiries within your first 30 days with us, we refund every penny you've paid us — no questions asked."

**Risk to agency:** High if the metric is shaky. Don't promise 5 leads if your average is 2.

---

## 2. Hybrid setup-refund + month-to-month

**Structure:** "Pay setup. We deliver [scoped first-30-days work]. If [metric] doesn't hit by day 30, refund setup and end engagement. After day 30, month-to-month, cancel anytime."

**When to use:** Most agencies. Reduces upfront risk; keeps operator un-exposed past the safety window.

**Sample copy:**
> "Pay the $2,500 setup and your first month. If you don't see at least 5 net-new patient inquiries by day 30, we refund the setup and unsubscribe you with no further obligation. After day 30, you're on month-to-month — cancel anytime."

**Risk to agency:** Bounded to setup fee + first month effort. Manageable.

---

## 3. Performance-based ("we don't get paid unless you do")

**Structure:** "Pay only when [outcome] hits."

**When to use:** Pure outcome work like paid ads where the metric is downstream and you have direct control. Aggressive but powerful.

**Sample copy:**
> "We don't bill until you've booked at least 10 calls from our outbound campaigns. After that, $500 per booked call from our work, capped at $5,000/mo."

**Risk to agency:** High — front-loaded work, deferred revenue. Reserve for offers where you can confidently hit the metric in 30 days and the deal-size justifies the float.

---

## 4. Double-your-money-back

**Structure:** "If we don't deliver [outcome], we pay you 2x your investment."

**When to use:** Almost never. Looks aggressive but reads as gimmicky. Skip.

**Sample copy:** Don't.

---

## 5. Work-for-free continuation

**Structure:** "If [outcome] doesn't hit by [date], we keep working at no cost until it does."

**When to use:** Long-arc outcomes (SEO, brand-building) where money-back doesn't make sense but extended labor does.

**Sample copy:**
> "If we don't move you into the top 5 organic results for [primary keyword] [city] within 9 months, we extend the engagement at no additional cost until we do."

**Risk to agency:** Time, not cash. Make sure the metric is one you can defensively hit with extra effort.

---

## 6. Right-to-cancel + prorated refund

**Structure:** "Cancel anytime. Prorated refund for unused service."

**When to use:** Foundational. Combines well with any of the above. Removes "lock-in" objection.

**Sample copy:**
> "Cancel anytime with 14 days' notice. We'll prorate your remaining month and refund the unused portion."

**Risk to agency:** Low. This is table stakes in 2026.

---

## 7. Capped engagement (the "we'll know in 90 days")

**Structure:** "Engagement is capped at 90 days. After day 90, we recommend whether to continue or part ways."

**When to use:** Risk-averse buyers. Reframes the engagement as a diagnostic, not an open-ended commitment.

**Sample copy:**
> "Our engagement is capped at 90 days. At day 90, we deliver a results review and either recommend continuing month-to-month or part ways with a clean handoff. No surprises, no auto-renewal."

**Risk to agency:** Forces you to deliver fast. Right pressure.

---

## 8. Asymmetric guarantee (the operator's-time-only)

**Structure:** "If [outcome] doesn't happen, we refund the cash but you keep the assets."

**When to use:** Asset-heavy work (sites, funnels, content libraries). Buyer keeps the work product even if the outcome lags.

**Sample copy:**
> "If we don't drive 50 new monthly inquiries within 6 months, we refund every dollar — and you keep the rebuilt site, the content library, and the ad accounts. The assets are yours regardless."

**Risk to agency:** Cash refund risk, but no IP loss. Buyer perceives extreme fairness; closes deals on bigger fish.

---

## Anti-guarantees (don't do these)

- **"100% satisfaction guaranteed."** Meaningless. Refund-on-demand bait.
- **"You'll love it or your money back."** Subjective metric = endless dispute.
- **Guarantees on metrics outside your control.** "We guarantee revenue lift" — revenue is downstream of your work AND their close rate / fulfillment / pricing. Guarantee leads, traffic, calls, rankings, content delivered. Don't guarantee revenue unless you're a paid-ads agency with full attribution.
- **Guarantees with fine print.** "Money back guarantee*" with 12 conditions. Buyer notices. Trust evaporates.

## How to choose

Match guarantee aggression to offer maturity:

- **Brand-new offer (under 5 paying clients):** Start with #2 (Hybrid setup-refund). Limits exposure while you tighten ops.
- **Mature offer (10+ paying clients, predictable metrics):** Move to #1 (Conditional money-back) or #5 (Work-for-free continuation). Bigger lever on close rate.
- **Premium offer (>$10k/mo):** Layer #6 (right to cancel) on top of any of the above. Removes the last objection.
- **Low-ticket offer (<$2k/mo):** #6 alone is usually enough. Aggressive guarantees on small deals don't move close rate much.

## When in doubt

If the operator hesitates to commit to a guarantee, that's information. Either the offer isn't tight yet (work levers 1-4 harder) or the operator isn't confident in delivery yet (build proof first, then design the offer).
